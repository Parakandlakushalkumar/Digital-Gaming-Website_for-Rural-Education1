Arjun woke up excited because today was the school's Science Fair, and he had to help set up heavy equipment. As he struggled to lift a big table, his grandfather showed him how to use a lever - a simple wooden plank placed on a fulcrum. "See how much easier it is now?" his grandfather smiled. Next, they used a wheel and axle (a trolley) to move boxes, an inclined plane (ramp) to load heavy items, and a pulley to hoist a banner. Arjun realized that simple machines don't create energy - they just make our work easier by changing the direction or amount of force we need to apply!

At the Science Fair, Arjun was amazed by the different energy sources on display. The solar panel demonstration showed how solar energy from the sun converts sunlight into electricity. Arjun learned that energy transformation happens everywhere - from the food we eat (chemical energy) to the electricity in our homes (electrical energy)!

The most exciting part for Arjun was the circuit-building workshop! He learned that an electric circuit is a complete path that allows electricity to flow. Arjun discovered that circuit design is everywhere: in our phones, computers, cars, and even LED street lights!
